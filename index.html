About Abbas Kiarostami



Writes Mike Leigh: "Here he is, one of the greatest of us all, the originator and master of the minimalist epic, the visionary who has raised the cinema of humanity to an unprecedented level of purity, the reluctant teacher who modestly confronts you with truths so profound that they will blind you with their luminous clairvoyance, the outrageous provocateur with an exhilarating capacity to make statements about his ideas and methods at which you will be so shocked that you will likely howl out loud, albeit joyfully."
Abbas Kiarostami was a pioneering Iranian filmmaker, photographer, and poet, recognized for his profound influence on contemporary Iranian cinema. Born on June 22, 1940, in Tehran, Kiarostami gained international acclaim in the 1990s with "Close-Up," known for his minimalist style and exploration of existential themes and human relationships.
He revolutionized storytelling with unconventional narrative structures, long takes, and naturalistic performances, often blending fiction and reality. His films, rich in universal themes like love and loss, used subtle gestures to evoke deep emotional responses.
Kiarostami's work extended to photography and poetry, integrating his visual and literary passions into his films. culture. His innovative approach and commitment to the human condition have left a lasting impact on global cinema, inspiring filmmakers worldwide. He passed away on July 4, 2016, but his legacy endures through his influential body of work. (Paraphrase: https://www.bookey.app/quote-author/abbas-kiarostami )


The Project


With this project, we propose to shed light on the peculiarity and troubles of an artist and his creative mind by connecting the person himself and a sample of the heterogeneous entities of his creation. To do that, we explored the possibilities offered by the technologies used in the Semantic Web, such as vocabularies, ontologies, RDF, and URIs. Therefore, the project aims to model an organized linked open-data environment that connects information categorized as concepts, items, people, places, and events about Rȃmi, in the most interoperable and semantically meaningful way possible.
