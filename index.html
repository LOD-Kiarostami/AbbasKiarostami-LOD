About Abbas Kiarostami



"Here he is, one of the greatest of us all, the originator and master of the minimalist epic, the visionary who has raised the cinema of humanity to an unprecedented level of purity, the reluctant teacher who modestly confronts you with truths so profound that they will blind you with their luminous clairvoyance, the outrageous provocateur with an exhilarating capacity to make statements about his ideas and methods at which you will be so shocked that you will likely howl out loud, albeit joyfully."
Mike Leigh

Abbas Kiarostami was a pioneering Iranian filmmaker, photographer, and poet, recognized for his profound influence on contemporary Iranian cinema. Born on June 22, 1940, in Tehran, Kiarostami gained international acclaim in the 1990s with Close-Up, 1990 known for his minimalist style and exploration of existential themes and human relationships.
He revolutionized storytelling with unconventional narrative structures, long takes, and naturalistic performances, often blending fiction and reality. His films, rich in universal themes like love and loss, used subtle gestures to evoke deep emotional responses.
Kiarostami's work extended to photography and poetry, integrating his visual and literary passions into his films. culture. His innovative approach and commitment to the human condition have left a lasting impact on global cinema, inspiring filmmakers worldwide. He passed away on July 4, 2016, but his legacy endures through his influential body of work.


A journey through the items


This section summarizes the ten items that were taken into consideration. They were selected according to relevance and heterogeneity criteria to present a range of entities related to the figure. They form a complex relationship network with the filmmaker at its center.

Taste of Cherry

Untitled, 2000

Where is Kiarostami
An event held by Cineteca on the peculiarities of Kiarostami as a filmmaker and photographer, discussing the recurring themes in his work.
Lessons with Kiarostami
A book in the first-person point of view on a series of workshops worldwide led by Kiarostami.
76 Minutes and 15 Seconds with Abbas Kiarostami
A documentary about the legendary filmmaker Abbas Kiarostami. A photographer and long-time friend of Kiarostami, Seyfolah Samadian has put together some footage of his moments with Kiarostami, his travel photography, filmmaking, etc.

A Wolf Lying in Wait
A compilation of the original and the translation of Kiarostami’s poetry in Haiku, focusing on nature, seasons, or moments of beauty, capturing a brief moment in time.
Kiarostami Directs
A 1996 Portrait of Kiarostami on the set of the movie Taste of Cherry

Knowledge Organization

After selecting the area of study and the 10 representative items, sourced from various institutions like libraries, archives, and museums, the initial phase of their analysis involves the Knowledge Organization Process. In this stage, the gathered data are systematically described and modeled, capturing different degrees of abstraction.

Conceptual Map

The Conceptual Map offers an initial overview of the descriptive process, illustrating all the data derived from the various items in natural language. It clearly outlines the relationships between these items and the central concept.





E/R Model


The Conceptual Map is converted into an E/R Model, a visual depiction that enables a more abstract description of the data. In this model, people, places, dates, and subjects are represented as Entities, while their interconnections are depicted as Relationships.






Metadata Analysis

The next phase involves revisiting the institutions and item providers to conduct a detailed analysis of the metadata they use for description. This entails identifying the standards and guidelines they adhere to. For those without a clear declaration, we used either the most commonly adopted standard for that type of institution or a more general metadata standard deemed appropriate for the specific provider.

Theoretical Model

Delving deeper into data interpretation occurs through the Theoretical Framework. Here, beginning with items, subjects, and relationships within the study domain, we searched the web to gather additional pertinent information. This aims to address the following inquiries:

  • Who: Enhancing insights into individuals and their roles and positions related to the study subject.
  • What: Describing objects, including their purposes, significance, or distinctive features.
  • Where: Characterizing places and significant locations.
  • When: Adding specifics about noteworthy dates or related events.


Enhanced E/R Model

The Theoretical Model, initially developed in natural language, is subsequently transformed into an Enhanced Entity/Relationship (E/R) Model. This refined version builds upon the previous E/R Model, incorporating the enhancements made during later stages of development.


Conceptual Model

A heightened level of abstraction is achieved in this phase, as we transition from the theoretical model to the formal representation offered by the Conceptual Model. Here, an ontological methodology guides us, where subjects and relationships outlined in the theoretical model are restructured using appropriate classes and properties derived from existing schemas, vocabularies, and ontologies.

These are the ontologies that we used:

  • crm: http://www.cidoc-crm.org/cidoc-crm/
  • dbo: http://dbpedia.org/ontology/
  • dcterms: http://purl.org/dc/terms/
  • frbroo: http://iflastandards.info/ns/fr/frbroo/
  • owl: http://www.w3.org/2002/07/owl#
  • rdfs: http://www.w3.org/2000/01/rdf-schema#
  • schema: https://schema.org/
  • time: http://www.w3.org/2006/time#

RDF Production

Three notable items sourced from various institutions were chosen as focal points for the RDF Production process. Initially, URIs were generated to describe key entities within these selected items. Subsequently, prefixes for the relevant ontologies were defined, and triples were constructed using Turtle serialization. These triples emphasize relationships among the items and their entities, aiming to link with other relevant resources and authorities wherever feasible.

Text Processing

                    
The book "A Wolf Lying in Wait: Selected Poems by Abbas Kiarostami" was chosen for text processing operations, and, a TEI/XML file for several pages of the book was prepared. Using Python and based
                        on an XSL stylesheet file, an HTML representation                        of this file was then generated. In the final task, a Python script was used to produce a Turtle/RDF output of the XML file. Since the selected text was a poem, additional triples were created from the TEIheader element of the TEI/XML file. The triples included the number of poems, language, genre,
                        and lines from the text to produce the RDF.
