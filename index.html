About Abbas Kiarostami



Ebrahim Monsefi, a poet, lyricist, and artist from the Southern region of Hormozgan in Iran, was born in 1945. Monsefi passed away in 1997, at the age of fifty-two. Modern Iran hears his lyrics through the melody of many musicians, yet his name falls into obscurity. Even though his passion for creative production ignited at a young age, his first and only music album was released 10 years after his death.
Monsefi chose the signature name of Rȃmi, but through his early usage of free verse in poetry he is called “The Nima of the South”. “Nima” refers to the name of the pioneer poet of “free verse” in the capital. Monsefi with a few of his contemporaries were the first individuals to turn to free verse poetry in the southern region, laying the groundwork for the continuation of this literary movement in the area. Being unquestionably influenced by “Ahmad Shamlou” in his free verse poems sometimes adds a distinctive touch to his poetry due to the influence of the local climate and geography.
Monsefi’s interest in Spanish flamenco and Indian music has influenced him to create highly admired works. He brought about a fundamental transformation in the genre of indigenous songwriting by leaning towards songs with social themes. His simple and unpretentious guitar-accompanied performances still attract numerous audiences and inspire young musicians.


The Project


With this project, we propose to shed light on the peculiarity and troubles of an artist and his creative mind by connecting the person himself and a sample of the heterogeneous entities of his creation. To do that, we explored the possibilities offered by the technologies used in the Semantic Web, such as vocabularies, ontologies, RDF, and URIs. Therefore, the project aims to model an organized linked open-data environment that connects information categorized as concepts, items, people, places, and events about Rȃmi, in the most interoperable and semantically meaningful way possible.

A journey through the items


This section summarizes the ten items that were taken into consideration. They were selected according to relevance and heterogeneity criteria to provide completeness to the project. They form a complex relationship network with the figure of Rȃmi at its center. An essential metadata, a short description, and a reference link are provided for each item.
